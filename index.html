Understanding Earth’s Layers

Earth is made up of distinct layers that each play a crucial role in supporting life, shaping landscapes, and creating the magnetic field that protects us. Explore each layer to learn more about our planet's fascinating inner structure.

↓

Crust

The Earth's crust is the outermost layer, composed of solid rock, minerals, and soil. It’s divided into two types: continental and oceanic crust.

Did you know? The crust makes up less than 1% of Earth’s volume but is essential for supporting life!

Mantle

The mantle lies below the crust, consisting of semi-solid rock that flows slowly over time. It is responsible for tectonic movements and volcanic activity.

Fun Fact: The mantle makes up about 84% of Earth’s volume and can reach temperatures of up to 4000°C.

Outer Core

The outer core is a layer of molten metal, primarily iron and nickel, creating Earth's magnetic field.

Interesting Insight: The outer core’s movement generates Earth's magnetic field, which protects us from harmful solar radiation.

Inner Core

The inner core is a dense, solid metal sphere composed mostly of iron and nickel.

Did you know? Despite its extreme heat, the inner core remains solid due to immense pressure.
